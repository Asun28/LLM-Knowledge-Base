## Findings

### MAJOR - src/kb/compile/linker.py:516

`inject_wikilinks_batch` does not fully re-derive the winning target from fresh under-lock content. The design requires the pre-lock scan to be candidate-gathering only, and under the page lock to re-read fresh body, re-scan for all batch titles, re-apply the deterministic `(-len(title), pid)` tie-break to current matches, and inject at most one link. The implementation instead iterates only over the stale pre-lock `candidates` list at `src/kb/compile/linker.py:516`. That means a title that becomes the correct fresh winner after the pre-lock read, but was not present in the stale snapshot, can never win. This violates AC1b's concurrency contract.

Suggested fix: under the lock, iterate `sorted_chunk` rather than `candidates`, applying the same self-link, `fresh_links`, and `title_patterns[title].search(body_masked)` filters to the fresh body before selecting the first winner. Keep the pre-lock candidate list only as a boolean lock gate, not as the authoritative winner universe.

### NIT - src/kb/compile/linker.py:358

The documented return shape contradicts the implementation. The docstring says targets with zero updates have empty lists, but targets with no candidate matches are absent. The implementation initializes `result` for every sanitized target at `src/kb/compile/linker.py:425`, so no-candidate targets are present with `[]`. The code shape is reasonable for callers because it distinguishes processed targets from skipped/invalid titles, but the docstring is inaccurate.

Suggested fix: update the docstring at `src/kb/compile/linker.py:356-359` to say every sanitized/processed `target_page_id.lower()` appears with a list that may be empty; skipped invalid/overlength titles are absent. Alternatively, if absence is the intended contract, stop preinitializing all targets and populate keys only when a target is touched.
